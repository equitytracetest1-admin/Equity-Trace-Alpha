Getting Started
5 topics
▶
How do I add my first trade?
▶
Click the + Add Trade button in the top-right corner, or press N on your keyboard. Fill in the required fields - symbol, direction, entry/exit prices, date, time, and P&L. Optional fields like setup, session, and notes help build better analytics over time.
How do I add an account?
▶
Go to Settings -> Accounts and click + Add Account. Enter your prop firm name, phase, account size, and drawdown limits. Each account is tracked independently and can be selected from the account switcher in the header.
Where is my data stored?
▶
All your data is stored locally in your browser using localStorage. Nothing is sent to any server. This means your data stays on your device - but also means clearing your browser data will erase it. Use the Export feature regularly to back up your trades.
How do I back up or transfer my data?
▶
Go to Settings -> Data and use the Export button to download your trades as a CSV or JSON file. To restore, use the Import button and select your backup file. To transfer to another device, export on the old device and import on the new one.
What does the version number mean?
▶
The app is currently in v1.0.0-beta. Beta means the core features are complete and stable, but some features are still being refined. You may encounter minor bugs - if you do, note what you were doing and report it.
📊
Dashboard & Overview
5 topics
▶
What does "All Accounts" mode show?
▶
When All Accounts is selected, the dashboard aggregates data across all your accounts - total P&L, combined win rate, and portfolio overview. The Daily P&L chart switches to dollar values instead of percentages since each account has a different size.
Why does the Profit Factor show infinity?
▶
Profit Factor = Total Wins ÷ Total Losses. If you have no losing trades (or haven't entered any losses yet), the denominator is zero - which results in ∞. This is mathematically correct and means your trading has been 100% profitable so far.
What is the Setup Scorecard?
▶
The Setup Scorecard ranks your trades by a composite score based on: win/loss result, risk management quality, kill zone alignment, instrument traded, and win streak multiplier. Higher scores indicate trades that were both profitable and well-executed. Hover over any row to see the score breakdown.
What are the Goals cards tracking?
▶
The Goals section shows three key prop firm metrics:
  • Max Loss - total drawdown limit from starting balance
  • Profit Target - amount needed to pass the evaluation
  • Daily Loss - maximum loss allowed in a single day, with a reset timer
These are configured per-account in Settings.
How does the Equity Curve work?
▶
The equity curve plots your running account balance after each trade. The dashed reference lines show your Profit Target (green), Initial Balance (grey), Daily Loss limit (amber), and Max Loss limit (red). Click any data point to open that trade's detail. Use the All / Month / Week filters to zoom in on different periods.

Trade Journal
5 topics
▶
How do I edit a trade?
▶
Click on any trade row in the Trade Journal to open the detail panel on the right. From there, click the Edit button (pencil icon) to open the trade in the edit modal. Make your changes and click Save Trade.
How do I add screenshots to a trade?
▶
Open a trade's detail panel, scroll to the Screenshots section. You can drag and drop images directly onto the upload area, paste from clipboard (Ctrl+V), or click to browse files. Each image can be labeled with a timeframe (1M, 5M, 1H, etc.) for organized chart review.
What is the Rating (stars) for?
▶
The star rating (1-5) is your personal quality assessment of how well you executed the trade - separate from whether it was profitable. A losing trade can still be 5-star if you followed your rules perfectly. Use this to track execution quality over time and identify patterns in well-rated vs poorly-rated trades.
How do I filter trades?
▶
Use the filter bar at the top of the Trade Journal. You can filter by:
  • Direction (Long / Short)
  • Result (Win / Loss / BE)
  • Symbol
  • Session (London, NY, Asia, etc.)
  • Date range
  • Account
Multiple filters can be combined. Click Clear to reset all filters.
What is Bulk Edit?
▶
Select multiple trades by clicking the checkboxes on the left of each row, then use the bulk action bar that appears at the bottom. You can bulk-update Account, Session, Setup, or Model fields across all selected trades at once - useful when importing trades that need categorization.

Analytics & Charts
4 topics
▶
What is the Heatmap showing?
▶
The calendar heatmap shows your daily P&L at a glance - green cells are profitable days, red cells are losing days, and the intensity of the color reflects the magnitude. Click any day to jump to that date in the Trade Journal.
What does the Daily P&L chart show?
▶
The horizontal bar chart shows your net P&L for each trading day of the selected week. Green bars extend right (profit), red bars extend left (loss). The X-axis shows percentages relative to your account size when a single account is selected, or dollar amounts when All Accounts is selected. Click a bar to filter the Trade Journal to that day.
What is the P&L by Session chart?
▶
This chart on the Advanced Analytics page breaks down your total P&L by trading session (London, New York, Asia, etc.). It helps you identify which sessions you perform best in and where you may be overtrading or underperforming.
How is R-Multiple calculated?
▶
R-Multiple (Reward-to-Risk) = Exit P&L ÷ Risk Amount. For example, if you risked $100 and made $150, your R-Multiple is 1.5R. To get accurate R values, enter your Stop Loss when logging trades. If no stop loss is entered, the app estimates based on average loss size.

Themes & Appearance
3 topics
▶
What are the three themes?
▶
  • Midnight - Pure black background, minimal and focused. Best for low-light environments.
  • Aura - Dark grey background with vivid accent-colored cards that glow. Best for a premium, vibrant look.
  • Dawn - Light background with accent-filled cards. Best for bright environments or daytime use.
Switch between them with the D key or from Settings.
My theme resets every time I open the app
▶
Theme preferences are saved in your browser's localStorage. If your browser is set to clear site data on close, or you're using private/incognito mode, settings won't persist. To fix this, allow the site to store data, or avoid incognito mode.

Prop Firm & Accounts
4 topics
▶
How do I set up a prop firm account?
▶
In Settings -> Accounts, click + Add Account. Fill in: Firm name, Phase (e.g. Phase 1 Eval, Funded), Account Size, Max Drawdown %, Daily Loss %, Profit Target %, and Drawdown Type (Trailing or Static). Once saved, it appears in the account switcher on the dashboard.
What is the difference between Trailing and Static drawdown?
▶
  • Static - Drawdown is calculated from your initial starting balance. The limit never changes regardless of profits.
  • Trailing - Drawdown limit follows your highest equity point. As you profit, the floor rises with you, making it progressively harder to breach.
Most prop firms use trailing drawdown during evaluations and static on funded accounts.
How does the Daily Loss timer reset work?
▶
The Daily Loss timer in the Goals card counts down to the next day reset - typically midnight in your local time or the prop firm's reset time (often 5 PM EST). Once reset, your daily P&L counter starts fresh. The timer shown is based on your local system clock.
Can I track multiple accounts at once?
▶
Yes. Add as many accounts as you like in Settings. Select All Accounts in the header to see aggregated portfolio stats, or select any individual account to drill into its specific metrics, goals, and trade history. The Portfolio Overview section on the dashboard shows all accounts side by side.

Troubleshooting
4 topics
▶
My data disappeared after opening the app
▶
This can happen if your browser cleared localStorage (e.g. after an update, in incognito mode, or due to browser settings). Always export your data regularly from Settings -> Data -> Export as a backup. If you have a backup, you can re-import it immediately.
Charts are not displaying correctly
▶
Try refreshing the page. If charts still appear broken, it may be due to a browser extension blocking scripts (e.g. ad blockers). Try disabling extensions temporarily, or open the app in a private window. Make sure you're using a modern browser (Chrome, Edge, or Firefox recommended).
The app looks different on my phone
▶
Equity Trace is optimized for desktop use. On mobile, the layout may be condensed - the sidebar collapses, and some panels may require scrolling. For the best experience, use a laptop or desktop with a minimum screen width of 900px.
A keyboard shortcut is not working
▶
Shortcuts are disabled when you're typing in an input field. Click on an empty area of the page first to make sure no input is focused. Also check that no modal or dialog is open - most shortcuts are blocked when a modal is active to prevent accidental actions.
